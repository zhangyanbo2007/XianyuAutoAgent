## Abstract

The superior colliculus (SC) is thought to integrate visual and auditory signals and help select actions, but it is unknown if and how these capabilities jointly contribute to behavior. Here, we show that the SC promotes competing actions independently of stimuli. We trained mice to locate an audiovisual stimulus on their left or right, and found that auditory, visual, and action-related signals are largely carried by separate SC neurons in different layers.
