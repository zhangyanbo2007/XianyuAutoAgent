原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

记忆还是检索：RAG感知预训练的缩放定律。检索增强生成（RAG）通过在测试时为知识密集型场景提供相关上下文来提高语言模型（LM）的性能。然而，在预训练期间获得的参数知识与通过检索访问的非参数知识之间的关系仍然知之甚少，尤其是在数据预算固定的情况下。在这项工作中，我们系统地研究了在广泛的模型和数据规模下，预训练语料库大小与检索存储大小之间的权衡。我们在高达1000亿个DCLM数据token上训练了基于OLMo-2的LM，其参数范围从30M到3B，同时改变预训练数据规模（参数数量的1-150倍）和检索存储大小（1-20倍），并在涵盖推理、科学问答和开放域问答的各种基准套件上评估了性能。我们发现，在各种模型规模下，检索始终比仅参数基线提高了性能，并引入了一个三维缩放框架，将性能建模为模型大小、预训练token和检索语料库大小的函数。这种缩放流形使我们能够估计在预训练和检索之间固定数据预算的最佳分配，揭示了检索的边际效用强烈依赖于模型规模、任务类型和预训练饱和度。我们的结果为理解检索何时以及如何补充预训练提供了定量基础，为可扩展语言建模系统设计中的数据资源分配提供了实用指导。

原始摘要

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) improves language model (LM) performance by providing relevant context at test time for knowledge-intensive situations. However, the relationship between parametric knowledge acquired during pretraining and non-parametric knowledge accessed via retrieval remains poorly understood, especially under fixed data budgets. In this work, we systematically study the trade-off between pretraining corpus size and retrieval store size across a wide range of model and data scales. We train OLMo-2-based LMs ranging from 30M to 3B parameters on up to 100B tokens of DCLM data, while varying both pretraining data scale (1–150× the number of parameters) and retrieval store size (1–20×), and evaluate performance across a diverse suite of benchmarks spanning reasoning, scientific QA, and open-domain QA. We find that retrieval consistently improves performance over parametric-only baselines across model scales and introduce a three-dimensional scaling framework that models performance as a function of model size, pretraining tokens, and retrieval corpus size. This scaling manifold enables us to estimate optimal allocations of a fixed data budget between pretraining and retrieval, revealing that the marginal utility of retrieval depends strongly on model scale, task type, and the degree of pretraining saturation. Our results provide a quantitative foundation for understanding when and how retrieval should complement pretraining, offering practical guidance for allocating data resources in the design of scalable language modeling systems.
