中文对照翻译

存货控制中的AI智能体：人、LLM与运筹学的互补性存货控制是一个基本运营问题，其中订购决策传统上由基于理论的运筹学（OR）算法指导。然而，此类算法通常依赖于僵化的建模假设，并且在需求分布发生变化或相关上下文信息不可用时，其性能可能不佳。大型语言模型（LLMs）的最新进展引发了人们对能够灵活推理并整合丰富上下文信号的AI智能体的兴趣，但目前尚不清楚如何最好地将基于LLM的方法整合到传统的决策流程中。我们研究了在多周期存货控制环境中，运筹学算法、LLMs和人类如何相互作用并互补。我们构建了InventoryBench，这是一个包含1,000多个存货实例的基准，涵盖合成和真实世界的需求数据，旨在在需求变化、季节性和不确定提前期下对决策规则进行压力测试。通过这个基准，我们发现运筹学增强的LLM方法优于任何单一方法，这表明这些方法是互补而非替代关系。我们通过一项受控的课堂实验进一步探讨了人类的作用，该实验将LLM的建议嵌入到人机协作的决策流程中。与先前认为人机协作可能降低性能的发现相反，我们表明，平均而言，人机团队比单独操作的人类或AI智能体获得更高的利润。除了这一群体层面的发现，我们还形式化了个体层面的互补效应，并推导出了从AI协作中受益的个体比例的无分布下限；经验上，我们发现这一比例是可观的。综上所述，我们的结果提供了证据，表明有效的存货管理受益于一个互补系统，其中运筹学提供启发式方法和计算器，LLMs贡献世界知识和上下文推理，而人类判断在自动化决策之外增加了价值。

原始摘要

Inventory control is a fundamental operations problem in which ordering decisions are traditionally guided by theoretically grounded operations research (OR) algorithms. However, such algorithms often rely on rigid modeling assumptions and can perform poorly when demand distributions shift or relevant contextual information is unavailable. Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have generated interest in AI agents that can reason flexibly and incorporate rich contextual signals, but it remains unclear how best to incorporate LLM-based methods into traditional decision-making pipelines. We study how OR algorithms, LLMs, and humans can interact and complement each other in a multi-period inventory control setting. We construct InventoryBench, a benchmark of over 1,000 inventory instances spanning both synthetic and real-world demand data, designed to stress-test decision rules under demand shifts, seasonality, and uncertain lead times. Through this benchmark, we find that OR-augmented LLM methods outperform either method in isolation, suggesting that these methods are complementary rather than substitutes. We further investigate the role of humans through a controlled classroom experiment that embeds LLM recommendations into a human-in-the-loop decision pipeline. Contrary to prior findings that human-AI collaboration can degrade performance, we show that, on average, human-AI teams achieve higher profits than either humans or AI agents operating alone. Beyond this population-level finding, we formalize an individual-level complementarity effect and derive a distribution-free lower bound on the fraction of individuals who benefit from AI collaboration; empirically, we find this fraction to be substantial. Taken together, our results provide evidence that effective inventory management benefits from a complementary system in which OR provides heuristics and calculators, LLMs contribute world knowledge and contextual reasoning, and human judgment adds value beyond automated decision making.
