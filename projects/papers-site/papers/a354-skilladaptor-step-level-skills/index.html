原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

大型语言模型（LLM）代理越来越依赖可重用外部技能来解决长时限交互式任务。现有的无训练技能适应管道通常从完整轨迹或会话级别反馈中更新技能，这使得失败归因粗糙，并且经常产生不稳定或过于宽泛的修订。我们提出了一种名为 S KILL A DAPTOR 的无训练、步级技能适应框架，具有明确的失败归因机制，并且可以插入 OpenClaw 类代理套件中。给定一个失败的轨迹，S KILL A DAPTOR 识别出第一个可操作的故障步骤，将责任链接到候选技能，并在明确接受检查下应用有针对性的更新，同时保持核心模型冻结。我们在 WebShop、PinchBench 和 Claw-Eval 上使用 Kimi-K2.5、GLM-5 和 GPT-5.2 进行了评估。S KIL L A DAPTOR 在这三个套件中均优于无技能和技能适应基线，在 PinchBench Avg Score% 上取得了最大的单个指标改进 +1.5 分，在 Claw-Eval Avg Score 上为 +1.8，在 WebShop success rate 上为 +1.7。这些结果表明，步级归因支持更稳定和可审计的无训练技能维护。

原始摘要

Large language model (LLM) agents increasingly rely on reusable external skills to solve long-horizon interactive tasks. Existing training-free skill adaptation pipelines usually update skills from full trajectories or sessionlevel feedback, which makes failure attribution coarse and often produces unstable or overly broad revisions. We propose S KILL A DAPTOR, a training-free step-level skill adaptation framework with explicit failure attribution, and it can plug into OpenClaw-class agent harnesses. Given a failed trajectory, S KILL A DAPTOR identifies a first actionable fault step, links responsibility to candidate skills, and applies targeted updates under explicit acceptance checks while keeping the backbone frozen. We evaluate on WebShop, PinchBench, and Claw-Eval with Kimi-K2.5, GLM-5, and GPT-5.2. S KIL L A DAPTOR improves over no-skill and skilladaptation baselines on all three suites, with the largest single-metric improvements of +1.5 points on PinchBench Avg Score%, +1.8 on Claw-Eval Avg Score, and +1.7 on WebShop success rate. These results indicate that steplevel attribution supports more stable and auditable training-free skill maintenance1 .
