原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

可解释的自驱动溅射外延：从黑箱优化到人类可用的生长规则。自驱动实验室已成为探索高维工艺空间的强大工具，然而，这些系统仍是黑箱优化器，产生的可迁移工艺理解有限。在本文中，我们展示了一个可解释的自驱动实验室框架，它将自主优化转化为人类可用的生长规则。作为一个严格的基准，我们将此框架应用于射频磁控溅射，解决了通过溅射实现高质量β-Ga2O3异质外延和单晶β-Ga2O3同质外延的长期挑战。通过将贝叶斯优化与Urbach能量的自动化光学评估（作为亚带隙无序的衡量标准）相结合，该自驱动系统有效地识别出异质外延生长条件，获得了182 meV的最小Urbach能量，这是溅射β-Ga2O3薄膜的最低值。重要的是，优化后的生长窗口是可迁移的，无需进一步优化即可实现单晶β-Ga2O3同质外延，这一点已通过扫描透射电子显微镜得到证实。为了将闭环数据集转化为可解释的生长规则，我们训练了一个随机森林代理模型，并将其提炼为响应曲线和四维生长参数空间中的量化成对相互作用。该分析确定衬底温度是主要的控制旋钮，射频功率和气体流量主要以加性方式作用，并且只有适度的温度-氧气耦合划定了高质量生长的狭窄窗口，从而建立了从自主实验到可迁移生长规则的通用途径。

原始摘要

Self-driving laboratories have emerged as powerful tools for navigating high-dimensional process spaces, yet systems remain black-box optimizers that yield limited transferable process understanding. Here, we demonstrate an interpretable self-driving laboratory framework that transforms autonomous optimization into human-usable growth rules. As a stringent benchmark, we apply this framework to RF magnetron sputtering, addressing a long-standing challenge of achieving high-quality β-Ga2O3 heteroepitaxy and singlecrystalline β-Ga2O3 homoepitaxy via sputtering. By combining Bayesian optimization with automated optical evaluation of the Urbach energy as a metric of sub-bandgap disorder, the self-driving system efficiently identifies heteroepitaxial growth conditions yielding a minimum Urbach energy of 182 meV, the lowest value for sputtered β-Ga2O3 films. Importantly, the optimized growth window is transferable, realizing singlecrystalline β-Ga2O3 homoepitaxy without further optimization, corroborated by scanning transmission electron microscopy. To convert the closed-loop dataset into interpretable growth rules, we train a random forest surrogate and distill it into response curves and quantified pairwise interactions across the four-dimensional growth-parameter space. This analysis identifies substrate temperature as the primary control knob, with RF power and gas flows acting largely additively and only a modest temperature-oxygen coupling delineating the narrow window for high-quality growth, establishing a general route from autonomous experimentation to transferable growth rules.
