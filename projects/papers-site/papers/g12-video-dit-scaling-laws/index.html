中文对照翻译

简单地扩大模型尺寸通常会带来性能提升。目前，最大的视频生成模型，如Movie Gen [45]，参数量已达到300亿，远超最初的视频DiT（约7亿）[39, 68]。然而，由于训练预算有限，无限扩大Transformer尺寸是不可行的。因此，在给定预算内确定最佳DiT尺寸已成为一个关键挑战。早期关于基于Transformer的语言模型的研究 [1, 12, 19, 26, 30] 表明，模型性能（通过验证损失 L 衡量）、模型大小 N 和给定计算预算 C 之间存在幂律关系，即缩放定律。这一发现使得可以使用较小的模型进行实验来预测较大模型的性能，从而在实际约束下选择最佳模型尺寸。在计算机视觉领域，研究人员主要对图像DiT的缩放特性 [33, 38, 41, 44, 47] 进行了经验性尝试，很少像语言模型那样建模精确的缩放定律。在视频生成领域，此类研究几乎不存在。鉴于视频模型的复杂性和重要性——它们不仅要建模视觉结构，还要模拟和理解时间维度上复杂的真实世界动态——它们的训练成本通常非常高；例如，Movie Gen 使用了6,144块H100 GPU进行训练 [45]。因此，为大规模视频生成模型开发准确实用的缩放定律尤为重要，因为选择正确的模型尺寸和训练配置可以大大降低成本。本研究旨在通过解决以下关键问题来探讨视频扩散Transformer的缩放定律：给定特定的视频Transformer架构和训练预算，我们如何预测最佳模型尺寸及其相应的性能和最佳超参数——特别是批次大小和学习率？我们通过应用一种常用的方法开始了我们的研究。

Currently, the largest video generation model like Movie Gen [45], has reached a parameter count of 30 billion, which is significantly larger than the initial video DiT around 700M [39, 68]. However, due to limited training budgets, infinitely scaling up transformer size is not feasible. Consequently, determining the optimal DiT size within a given budget has become a critical challenge. Early research on transformerbased language models [1, 12, 19, 26, 30] revealed that model performance (measured by validation loss L), the model size N , and the given compute budget C, exhibit a power-law relationship, known as scaling laws. This discovery enables the use of smaller models for experiments to predict the performance of larger models, thereby enabling the selection of the optimal model size under realworld constrains. In the field of computer vision, researchers have primarily made empirical attempts in the scaling properties [33, 38, 41, 44, 47] of image DiT, seldom modeling precise scaling laws as done in language models. In the domain of video generation, such research is virtually nonexistent. Given the complexity and importance of video models—which must not only model visual structures but also simulate and understand complex real-world dynamics over time dimension—they are often trained at very high costs; for example, Movie Gen was trained using 6,144 H100 GPUs [45]. Therefore, developing accurate and practical scaling laws for large-scale video generation models is particularly important, as selecting the correct model size and training configurations can greatly reduce costs. This study aims to investigate the scaling laws of video diffusion transformers by addressing the following key question: Given a specific video transformer architecture and training budget, how can we predict the optimal model size, along with its corresponding performance and optimal hyperparameters—specifically, batch size and learning rate?
