中文对照翻译

视觉-语言-动作 (VLA) 模型广泛采用预训练的视觉-语言模型 (VLM) 作为策略骨干模型，但目前尚不清楚哪种预训练 VLM 表示形式对 VLA 初始化有用。在本文中，我们沿着三个轴线将 VLA 初始化作为一个受控的表示设计问题进行研究：能力级具身 VQA 监督、参数更新策略和机器人数据预训练。我们的实验表明，原始预训练 VLM 表示是动作性能的关键来源。然而，具身 VQA 适应并不能带来统一的收益：它的益处取决于下游瓶颈，并且来自不同能力领域的收益并非简单叠加。对于更新策略，LoRA 提供了比完全微调更可靠的初始化，这表明过度重塑预训练表示会削弱 VLA 初始化。机器人数据预训练进一步改善了 VLA 初始化，通过分阶段的基于 LoRA 的训练获得了最强的变体。总而言之，这些发现表明，有效的 VLM 到 VLA 适应应该注入与动作相关的具身和机器人轨迹信号，同时保留对动作学习仍然有用的预训练 VLM 表示。代码可在以下网址获取：https://github.com/AFeng-x/Rethink_VLA_Initialization

原始摘要

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models widely adopt pretrained Vision-Language Models (VLMs) as policy backbones, yet it remains unclear what kind of pretrained VLM representation is useful as a VLA initialization. In this paper, we study VLA initialization as a controlled representation-design problem along three axes: capability-level embodied VQA supervision, parameter-update strategy, and robot-data pretraining. Our experiments show that the original pretrained VLM representation is a key source of action performance. However, embodied VQA adaptation does not yield uniform gains: its benefit depends on downstream bottlenecks, and gains from different capability domains are not simply additive. For update strategy, LoRA provides a more reliable initialization than Full Finetune, indicating that overly reshaping the pretrained representation can weaken VLA initialization. Robot-data pretraining further improves VLA initialization, with the strongest variant obtained by staged LoRA-based training. Together, these findings suggest that effective VLM-to-VLA adaptation should inject actionrelevant embodied and robot-trajectory signals while preserving the pretrained VLM representation that remains useful for action learning.
