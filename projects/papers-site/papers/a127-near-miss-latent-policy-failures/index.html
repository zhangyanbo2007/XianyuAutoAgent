中文对照翻译

近失：代理工作流中潜在策略失败检测。用于业务流程自动化的代理系统通常需要遵守管理系统状态条件更新的策略。在基于LLM的代理工作流中，策略遵守情况的评估通常通过将最终系统状态与预定义的真实情况进行比较来执行。尽管这种方法可以检测到明确的策略违规，但它可能会忽略一类更微妙的问题，即代理绕过了必需的策略检查，但由于有利的环境而达到了正确的结果。我们将此类情况称为近失或潜在失败。在这项工作中，我们引入了一种新颖的度量标准，用于检测代理对话轨迹中的潜在策略失败。我们的方法基于ToolGuard框架，该框架将自然语言策略转换为可执行的守卫代码，通过分析代理轨迹来确定代理的工具调用决策是否充分知情。我们在τ 2 -verified Airlines基准上，使用多个充当代理的当代开源和专有LLM评估了我们的方法。我们的结果表明，即使最终结果与预期的真实状态相符，在涉及修改性工具调用的轨迹中，潜在失败仍发生在8–17%的案例中。这些发现揭示了当前评估方法中的一个盲点，并强调了需要不仅评估最终结果，还要评估导致这些结果的决策过程的度量标准。

原始摘要

Agentic systems for business process automation often require compliance with policies governing conditional updates to the system state. Evaluation of policy adherence in LLM-based agentic workflows is typically performed by comparing the final system state against a predefined ground truth. While this approach detects explicit policy violations, it may overlook a more subtle class of issues in which agents bypass required policy checks, yet reach a correct outcome due to favorable circumstances. We refer to such cases as near-misses or latent failures. In this work, we introduce a novel metric for detecting latent policy failures in agent conversations traces. Building on the ToolGuard framework, which converts naturallanguage policies into executable guard code, our method analyzes agent trajectories to determine whether agent’s tool-calling decisions where sufficiently informed. We evaluate our approach on the τ 2 -verified Airlines benchmark across several contemporary open and proprietary LLMs acting as agents. Our results show that latent failures occur in 8–17% of trajectories involving mutating tool calls, even when the final outcome matches the expected ground-truth state. These findings reveal a blind spot in current evaluation methodologies and highlight the need for metrics that assess not only final outcomes but also the decision process leading to them.
