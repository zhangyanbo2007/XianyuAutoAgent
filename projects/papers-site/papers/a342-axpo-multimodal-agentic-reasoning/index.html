原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

具有扩展推理能力的视觉语言模型在复杂问题上取得了成功，但许多现实世界问题需要外部工具，而仅凭内部推理往往无法解决。因此，智能体推理将两种行为交织在一起，它们具有结构不对称性：思考（自包含的默认行为）和工具使用（高方差的辅助行为）。我们将这种不对称性称为“思考-行动差距”。在像GRPO这样的标准强化学习方法下，这种差距在训练期间表现为两种诊断症状：工具使用仅在约30%的rollout中尝试，并且当尝试时，一个组内使用工具的rollout在约40%的问题上完全错误，从而抑制了在需要工具调用的地方的学习信号。我们提出了AXPO（Agent eXplorative Policy Optimization）：对于每个完全错误的工具使用子组，AXPO固定思考前缀并重新采样工具调用及其后续，并结合基于不确定性的前缀选择。在九个多模态基准测试和三个规模的Qwen3-VL-Thinking上，SFT + AXPO平均优于SFT + GRPO（平均在8B上Pass@1提高1.8个百分点，Pass@4提高1.8个百分点），并且8B的SFT + AXPO在Pass@4上超越了32B Base，参数量减少了4倍。项目页面可在链接处获取。

原始摘要

Vision-language models with extended reasoning succeed on complex problems, but many real-world problems require external tools that internal reasoning alone often cannot resolve. Agentic reasoning therefore interleaves two behaviors with a structural asymmetry: thinking (the self-contained default) and tool use (a high-variance auxiliary acting). We refer to this asymmetry as the Thinking-Acting Gap. Under standard RL recipes like GRPO, the gap manifests as two diagnostic symptoms during training: tool use is attempted on only ∼30% of rollouts, and when attempted, the tool-using rollouts within a group are all-wrong on ∼40% of questions, suppressing the learning signal at the tool calls that needed it. We propose AXPO (Agent eXplorative Policy Optimization): for each all-wrong tool-using subgroup, AXPO fixes the thinking prefix and resamples the tool call and its continuation, paired with uncertainty-based prefix selection. Across nine multimodal benchmarks and three scales of Qwen3-VL-Thinking, SFT + AXPO outperforms SFT + GRPO at average (+1.8 pp Pass@1 and +1.8 pp Pass@4 at 8B on average) and 8B with SFT + AXPO surpasses the 32B Base on Pass@4 with 4× fewer parameters.
