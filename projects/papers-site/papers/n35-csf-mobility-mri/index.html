问题与背景

Many neurological diseases involve impaired clearance of toxic proteins, but the forces that drive cerebrospinal fluid mobility in human perivascular spaces remain debated. This paper addresses that gap with a CSF-specific MRI measurement framework in living humans.

方法/新意

The core contribution is a noninvasive MRI approach that isolates and quantifies CSF mobility region by region, enabling the authors to test which anatomical and physiological factors drive clearance-related flow in vivo rather than inferring it indirectly.

意义/放在仓库中的位置

This belongs on the cognitive and systems neuroscience mainline. It is a strong mechanistic measurement paper with translational relevance to neurodegeneration and human brain physiology, even though it is not directly an AI paper.

局限/为何不更高

Its contribution is primarily a strong biological measurement and mechanism result. It does not establish a new cross-domain computational paradigm, so it fits best as breakthrough.
