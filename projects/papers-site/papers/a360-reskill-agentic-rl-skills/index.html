原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

代理强化学习 (RL) 允许大型语言模型 (LLM) 持续从环境奖励中改进，但由此产生的策略并未系统地积累可重用策略，这些策略能够泛化到不同的任务。模块化技能可以提供这些可重用的策略，但现有的技能增强强化学习方法将技能创建与策略优化解耦，从而可能采用与不断演变的策略冲突的技能。受到 Anthropic 的 Skill Creator 的启发，我们引入了 R E S KILL，这是一个 RL-in-the-loop 的技能创建框架，它将技能演化与策略学习相结合。R E S KILL 利用 GRPO 的组间结构，仅在可忽略不计的额外开销下，自然地嵌入了三种机制：(1) 驱动式技能创建器，它通过从过去经验中诊断失败来提出条件、基于触发器的技能修订；(2) 组内滚动采样，它允许对技能版本进行受控比较，从而捕捉哪个版本最好地支持策略的持续学习；以及 (3) 带有自适应折扣的 Thompson Sampling，用于在策略演化过程中平衡技能版本选择中的探索与利用。在多个领域，R E S KILL 始终优于现有的记忆和基于技能的强化学习方法，尤其是在未见任务上取得了最大的收益。技能生命周期的分析表明，随着策略的改进，技能会自动创建、测试、完善和删除，从而展示了技能与策略的协同演化。

原始摘要

Agentic reinforcement learning (RL) enables LLM agents to improve continuously from environment rewards, yet the resulting policies do not systematically accumulate reusable strategies that generalize across tasks. Modular skills can provide such reusable strategies, yet existing skill-augmented RL methods decouple skill creation from policy optimization, risking adopting skills that conflict with the evolving policy. Inspired by Anthropic’s Skill Creator, we introduce R E S KILL, an RL-in-the-loop skill creation framework that reconciles skill evolution with policy learning. R E S KILL exploits the group-wise structure of GRPO to naturally embed three mechanisms with only marginal additional overhead: (1) an assertion-driven skill creator that diagnoses failures from past experience and proposes conditional, trigger-based skill revisions; (2) within-group rollout sampling that enables controlled comparison of skill versions, capturing which version best supports the policy’s ongoing learning; and (3) Thompson Sampling with adaptive discounting to balance exploration and exploitation in skill version selection as the policy evolves. Across several domains, R E S KILL consistently outperforms existing memory and skill-based RL methods, with the largest gains on unseen tasks. Analysis of the skill lifecycle shows skills being automatically created, tested, refined, and pruned as the policy improves, demonstrating reconciled skill-policy co-evolution.
