原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

RLVR提升了大型语言模型（LLM）的推理能力，但其有效性常因难题上严重的奖励稀疏性而受限。最近基于提示的强化学习方法通过注入部分解决方案或抽象模板来缓解稀疏性，但它们通常通过增加更多token来扩展指导，这引入了冗余、不一致性和额外的训练开销。我们提出了KnowRL（知识引导强化学习），一个将提示设计视为最小充分指导问题的强化学习训练框架。在强化学习训练期间，KnowRL将指导分解为原子知识点（KPs），并使用约束子集搜索（CSS）来构建紧凑、交互感知的训练子集。我们进一步识别了一个剪枝交互悖论——移除一个知识点可能有所帮助，而移除多个此类知识点则可能有害——并在此依赖结构下明确优化鲁棒的子集筛选。我们从OpenMath-Nemotron-1.5B训练了KnowRL-Nemotron1.5B。在1.5B规模的八个推理基准测试中，KnowRL-Nemotron-1.5B始终优于强大的强化学习和提示基线。在推理时没有知识点提示的情况下，KnowRL-Nemotron1.5B达到了70.08的平均准确率，已经比Nemotron-1.5B高出9.63个百分点；通过选定的知识点，性能提高到74.16，在该规模下建立了新的最先进水平。模型、精选训练数据和代码已在https://github.com/Hasuer/KnowRL公开。

原始摘要

RLVR improves reasoning in large language models, but its effectiveness is often limited by severe reward sparsity on hard problems. Recent hint-based RL methods mitigate sparsity by injecting partial solutions or abstract templates, yet they typically scale guidance by adding more tokens, which introduce redundancy, inconsistency, and extra training overhead. We propose KnowRL (Knowledge-Guided Reinforcement Learning), an RL training framework that treats hint design as a minimal-sufficient guidance problem. During RL training, KnowRL decomposes guidance into atomic knowledge points (KPs) and uses Constrained Subset Search (CSS) to construct compact, interactionaware subsets for training. We further identify a pruning interaction paradox—removing one KP may help while removing multiple such KPs can hurt—and explicitly optimize for robust subset curation under this dependency structure. We train KnowRL-Nemotron1.5B from OpenMath-Nemotron-1.5B. Across eight reasoning benchmarks at the 1.5B scale, KnowRL-Nemotron-1.5B consistently outperforms strong RL and hinting baselines. Without KP hints at inference, KnowRL-Nemotron1.5B reaches 70.08 average accuracy, already surpassing Nemotron-1.5B by +9.63 points; with selected KPs, performance improves to 74.16, establishing a new state of the art at this scale. The model, curated training data, and code are publicly available at https:// github.com/Hasuer/KnowRL.
