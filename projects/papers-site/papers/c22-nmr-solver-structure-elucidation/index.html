核磁共振（NMR）光谱学是有机化学中用于分子结构解析的最强大和最广泛使用的工具之一。然而，解释核磁共振谱图以确定未知分子结构仍然是一个劳动密集型且依赖专业知识的过程，特别是对于复杂或新型化合物。尽管最近提出了用于分子结构解析的方法，但由于固有的算法局限性和有限的高质量数据，它们在实际应用中往往表现不佳。在本文中，我们提出了NMR-Solver，这是一个实用且可解释的框架，用于基于H和C核磁共振谱图自动确定小有机分子结构。我们的方法引入了一个用于分子结构解析的自动化框架，该框架整合了大规模谱图匹配与物理引导的分子优化，利用了核磁共振中的原子级结构-谱图关系。我们在模拟基准、文献中精选的实验数据和实际实验中评估了NMR-Solver，证明了其在实际场景中的强大泛化能力、鲁棒性和实用性。通过将计算核磁共振分析、深度学习和可解释的化学推理整合到一个统一系统中，它促进了可扩展、自动化且具有化学意义的分子结构解析，为解决分子科学中的逆问题建立了可泛化的范式。

原始摘要

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) spectroscopy is one of the most powerful and widely used tools for molecular structure elucidation in organic chemistry. However, the interpretation of NMR spectra to determine unknown molecular structures remains a labor-intensive and expertise-dependent process, particularly for complex or novel compounds. Although recent methods have been proposed for molecular structure elucidation, they often underperform in real-world applications due to inherent algorithmic limitations and limited high-quality data. Here, we present NMR-Solver, a practical and interpretable framework for the automated determination of small organic molecule structures from H and C NMR spectra. Our method introduces an automated framework for molecular structure elucidation, integrating large-scale spectral matching with physics-guided molecular optimization that exploits atomic-level structure–spectrum relationships in NMR. We evaluate NMR-Solver on simulated benchmarks, curated experimental data from the literature, and real-world experiments, demonstrating its strong generalization, robustness, and practical utility in real-life scenarios. By integrating computational NMR analysis, deep learning, and interpretable chemical reasoning into a unified system, it facilitates scalable, automated, and chemically meaningful molecular structure elucidation, establishing a generalizable paradigm for solving inverse problems in molecular science.
