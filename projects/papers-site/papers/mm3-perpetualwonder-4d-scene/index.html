核心要点

问题/背景
这篇论文想解决的是单图出发的长时程 4D 场景生成问题。现有很多视频生成方法能生成“看起来像运动”的结果，但物理状态和视觉表示是分裂的，导致时间一长就失真，更谈不上真正的 action-conditioned 交互场景。
方法/机制
方法上，PerpetualWonder 把物理仿真和视频生成做成闭环：前向依赖物理状态推进，后向通过神经优化同时修正视觉外观和动力学一致性。它还支持多视角 refinement 和更长时间范围的 4D scene update，目标不是一段局部视频，而是一个可持续演化的场景表示。
结果/证据
这篇工作的意义在于，它把 4D 生成、世界模型和物理仿真三条线真正接起来了。对生成式建模来说，这种“physics in the loop”的做法比普通视频生成更接近具身智能和世界建模需求，因此很适合放进仓库主线。
收录价值

1. 引言 近年来，文本、图像和视频的生成模型取得了显著进展。这一快速发展推动了生成式世界模型[4, 15, 39]的创建，这些模型对于VR/AR、游戏和具身AI[30]中的应用至关重要。这类模型的一个关键能力不仅是生成逼真的内容，还在于模拟一个能响应用户动作的世界。我们研究从单张图像生成动作条件下的4D场景的任务。给定一张输入图像和一系列物理动作（局部力如推和戳，或全局力如风场和重力），目标是生成与这些动作相对应并随时间合理演变的动态4D场景。早期响应动作生成4D内容的尝试严重依赖于传统的物理模拟[6, 14, 48, 58]。这些方法虽然提供了精确且可解释的物理控制，但其动力学和外观完全由传统模拟器驱动。这通常会导致显著的真实感差距，因为简化的物理和解析渲染难以捕捉复杂的视觉效果。我们引入了PerpetualWonder，这是一种混合生成模拟器，能够从单张图像实现长程、动作条件下的4D场景生成。当前的工作在此任务上表现不佳，因为它们的物理状态与其视觉表示是解耦的，这阻碍了生成式改进来更新后续交互的底层物理。PerpetualWonder通过引入第一个真正的闭环系统解决了这个问题。它采用了一种新颖的统一表示，在物理状态和视觉基元之间建立了双向链接，从而允许生成式改进同时修正动力学和外观。它还引入了一种鲁棒的更新机制，该机制从多个视角收集监督信息以解决优化模糊性。实验表明，PerpetualWonder能够从单张图像成功模拟长程动作产生的复杂多步交互，同时保持物理合理性和视觉一致性。* 共同贡献

原始摘要

1. Introduction Recent years have seen remarkable progress in generative models for text, images, and videos. This rapid advancement motivates the creation of generative world models [4, 15, 39], which are crucial for applications in VR/AR, gaming, and embodied AI [30]. A key capability for such models is not just to generate realistic content, but to simulate a world that responds to user actions. We study the task of action-conditioned 4D scene generation from a single image. Given a single input image and a sequence of physical actions (local forces like pushes and pokes, or global forces like wind fields and gravity), the goal is to generate the dynamic 4D scene that corresponds to the actions and evolves plausibly over time. Early attempts to generate 4D content in response to actions relied heavily on traditional physical simulation [6, 14, 48, 58]. These methods, while offering precise and interpretable physical control, are driven entirely by the traditional simulator for both dynamics and appearance. This often results in a significant realism gap, as simplified physics and analytic rendering struggle to capture the complex vi- We introduce PerpetualWonder, a hybrid generative simulator that enables long-horizon, action-conditioned 4D scene generation from a single image. Current works fail at this task because their physical state is decoupled from their visual representation, which prevents generative refinements to update the underlying physics for subsequent interactions. PerpetualWonder solves this by introducing the first true closed-loop system. It features a novel unified representation that creates a bidirectional link between the physical state and visual primitives, allowing generative refinements to correct both the dynamics and appearance. It also introduces a robust update mechanism that gathers supervision from multiple viewpoints to resolve optimization ambiguity. Experiments demonstrate that from a single image, PerpetualWonder can successfully simulate complex, multi-step interactions from long-horizon actions, maintaining physical plausibility and visual consistency. * Equal contribution
