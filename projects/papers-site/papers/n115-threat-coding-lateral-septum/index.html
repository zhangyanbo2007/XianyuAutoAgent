原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

外侧隔核中特征特异性威胁编码指导防御行为。快速检测和评估潜在威胁的能力对于生存至关重要，它需要将感觉信息与内部状态和先前经验相结合。外侧隔核（LS）——边缘前脑中的一个抑制性结构——被认为整合这些高阶认知信号以调节防御反应。然而，作为这一过程基础的细胞、回路和计算机制仍然未知。在这里，我们关注表达2型CRH受体的外侧隔核神经元群（LS），该神经元亚群已被证明对小鼠的状态依赖性行为变化和威胁反应性至关重要。我们结合使用单细胞钙成像、分子测序和回路解剖，以揭示所涉及细胞类型的空间和功能组织、它们执行的计算以及其上游激活器传递的信息。我们确定外侧隔核群体活动通过快速动态编码预测行为结果的威胁表征，对于线索驱动的防御行为是必需的。我们发现这些威胁表征是通过不同外侧隔核亚类差异性表示的各种信号的汇聚而形成的，这些亚类由其分子特征、空间位置和输入架构定义。值得注意的是，这些反应反映了来自海马体和下丘脑的特定传入神经，它们分别优先传递线索相关和行动相关信号。这些发现建立了一个多特征组织原则，阐明了外侧隔核如何响应环境挑战来介导动机行为。

原始摘要

The ability to rapidly detect and evaluate potential threats is essential for survival and requires the integration of sensory information with internal state and previous experience. The lateral septum (LS)—an inhibitory structure in the limbic forebrain—is thought to integrate these higher-order cognitive signals to regulate defensive responses . However, the cellular, circuit and computational mechanisms fundamental to this process remain unknown. Here we focus on the population of LS neurons that express the type 2 CRH receptor (LS ), a neuronal subset shown to be critical for state-dependent behavioural changes and threat responsivity in mice. We use a combination of single-cell calcium imaging, molecular sequencing and circuit dissection to reveal the spatial and functional organization of the cell types involved, the computations they perform and the information relayed by their upstream activators. We determine that LS population activity is required for cue-driven defensive actions by rapidly and dynamically encoding threat representations that predict behavioural outcomes. We find that these threat representations are formed through the convergence of various signals differentially represented by distinct LS subclasses, which are defined by their molecular features, spatial locations and input architectures. Notably, these responses reflect specific afferents from the hippocampus and hypothalamus that preferentially impart cue- and action-related signals, respectively. These findings establish a multifeatured organizational principle that underlies how the LS mediates motivated behaviours in response to environmental challenges.
