中文对照翻译

大型语言模型（LLMs）的最新进展使其在推理和环境交互任务上表现出色，但其创造性问题解决能力仍未得到充分探索。我们通过创造性工具使用的视角研究了这种能力，即模型通过推理可用对象的“可供性”（affordances）和属性来重新利用它们，而不是依赖于其规范用法。作为第一步，我们引入了CreativityBench，一个用于评估LLMs中基于可供性的创造力的基准。为此，我们构建了一个包含4K实体和150K+可供性标注的大规模可供性知识库（KB），明确链接了对象、部件、属性和可操作用途。在此知识库的基础上，我们生成了14K个基于现实的任务，这些任务要求在约束条件下识别非显而易见但物理上合理的解决方案。对包括封闭和开源模型在内的10个最先进LLMs的评估表明，模型通常可以选择一个合理的物体，但未能识别正确的部件、它们的可供性以及解决任务所需的潜在物理机制，导致性能显著下降。此外，模型扩展带来的改进迅速饱和，强大的通用推理能力并不能可靠地转化为创造性的可供性发现，并且常见的推理时策略（如思维链Chain-of-Thought）带来的收益有限。这些结果表明，创造性工具使用仍然是当前模型面临的一个主要挑战，并且CreativityBench为研究智能的这一缺失维度提供了一个有用的试验台，对未来代理中的规划和推理模块具有潜在影响。代码

原始摘要

Recent advances in large language models have led to strong performance on reasoning and environment-interaction tasks, yet their ability for creative problemsolving remains underexplored. We study this capability through the lens of creative tool use, where a model repurposes available objects by reasoning about their affordances and attributes rather than relying on canonical usage. As a first step, we introduce CreativityBench, a benchmark for evaluating affordance-based creativity in LLMs. To this end, we build a large-scale affordance knowledge base (KB) with 4K entities and 150K+ affordance annotations, explicitly linking objects, parts, attributes, and actionable uses. Building on this KB, we generate 14K grounded tasks that require identifying non-obvious yet physically plausible solutions under constraints. Evaluations across 10 state-of-the-art LLMs, including closed and open-source models, show that models can often select a plausible object, but fail to identify the correct parts, their affordances, and the underlying physical mechanism needed to solve the task, leading to a significant drop in performance. Furthermore, improvements from model scaling quickly saturate, strong general reasoning does not reliably translate to creative affordance discovery, and common inference-time strategies such as Chain-of-Thought yield limited gains. These results suggest that creative tool use remains a major challenge for current models, and that CreativityBench provides a useful testbed for studying this missing dimension of intelligence, with potential implications for planning and reasoning modules in future agents.
