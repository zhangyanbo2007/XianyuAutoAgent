原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

自主AI研究进展迅速，但长周期机器学习研究工程仍然困难：代理需要在数小时或数天内，在任务理解、环境设置、实现、实验和调试等环节保持连贯的进展。我们引入了AiScientist，一个用于机器学习研究的自主长周期工程系统，它建立在一个简单原则之上：强大的长周期性能需要结构化编排和持久状态连续性。为此，AiScientist将分层编排与一个权限范围的“文件即总线”（File-as-Bus）工作空间相结合：一个顶层编排器通过简洁的摘要和工作空间地图来维持阶段级控制，而专业代理则反复基于分析、计划、代码和实验证据等持久性工件进行重新定位，而不是主要依赖对话式交接，从而实现对厚重状态的轻量级控制。在两个互补的基准测试中，AiScientist的PaperBench分数平均比最佳匹配基线提高了10.54分，并在MLE-Bench Lite上达到了81.82%的Any Medal%。消融研究进一步表明，File-as-Bus协议是性能的关键驱动因素，移除后PaperBench分数降低了6.41分，MLE-Bench Lite降低了31.82分。这些结果表明，长周期机器学习研究工程是一个在持久项目状态上协调专业工作的系统问题，而非一个纯粹的局部推理问题。∗ 贡献相同。† 通讯作者。

原始摘要

Autonomous AI research has advanced rapidly, but long-horizon ML research engineering remains difficult: agents must sustain coherent progress across task comprehension, environment setup, implementation, experimentation, and debugging over hours or days. We introduce AiScientist, a system for autonomous long-horizon engineering for ML research built on a simple principle: strong long-horizon performance requires both structured orchestration and durable state continuity. To this end, AiScientist combines hierarchical orchestration with a permission-scoped File-as-Bus workspace: a top-level Orchestrator maintains stage-level control through concise summaries and a workspace map, while specialized agents repeatedly re-ground on durable artifacts such as analyses, plans, code, and experimental evidence rather than relying primarily on conversational handoffs, yielding thin control over thick state. Across two complementary benchmarks, AiScientist improves PaperBench score by 10.54 points on average over the best matched baseline and achieves 81.82 Any Medal% on MLE-Bench Lite. Ablation studies further show that File-as-Bus protocol is a key driver of performance, reducing PaperBench by 6.41 points and MLE-Bench Lite by 31.82 points when removed. These results suggest that long-horizon ML research engineering is a systems problem of coordinating specialized work over durable project state, rather than a purely local reasoning problem. ∗ Equal Contributions. † Corresponding authors.
