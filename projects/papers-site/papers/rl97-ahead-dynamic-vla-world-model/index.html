原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

视觉-语言-动作 (VLA) 模型在静态操作中具有泛化能力，但在任务执行过程中遇到物体移动时会失败。它们将当前观察结果映射到动作，并假设观察与执行之间场景是静止的，因此在任何非平凡的物体速度下，结果延迟超过了抓取的时间。我们通过 AHEAD (基于自适应动力学的预测-然后-行动包装器) 填补这一差距，该包装器增强了冻结的 VLA，并包含了一个具有感知运动的潜在世界模型。一个在操作视频上训练的小世界模型预测了 VLA 特征空间中的未来补丁令牌，这些预测基于每个令牌的光流速度和加速度。一种语言和运动显著性掩码将预测集中在与任务相关的补丁上，并且模型以自适应地前进的方式进行滚动，当预测不确定性超过阈值时停止。冻结的动作解码器接收预测的未来令牌，而不是当前的令牌。AHEAD 为冻结的 7B OpenVLA 添加了 4.9M 个参数，并在 20 个动态模拟场景中实现了 79% 到 97% 的成功率，而最强基线在 31% 到 58% 之间。在物理 UFactory xArm 7 上，AHEAD 在三个传送带和滚动球任务中成功率为 29/30 到 30/30，在拍球拦截任务中成功率为 23/30，在投球捕获任务中成功率为 19/30，而所有基线都为 0/30。关键词：视觉-语言-动作模型，世界模型，操作。

原始摘要

Abstract: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models generalize across static manipulation but fail when objects move during task execution. They map the current observation to an action and assume the scene is stationary between observation and execution, so at any non-trivial object speed the resulting latency exceeds the time available to grasp. We close this gap with AHEAD (Anticipatory Horizon Extrapolation with Adaptive Dynamics), a predict-then-act wrapper that augments a frozen VLA with a motion-aware latent world model. A small world model trained on manipulation video forecasts future patch tokens in the VLA’s feature space, conditioned on per-token velocity and acceleration from optical flow. A language-and-motion saliency mask concentrates prediction on task-relevant patches, and the model rolls forward for an adaptive horizon, halting when prediction uncertainty crosses a threshold. The frozen action decoder then receives the predicted future tokens in place of the current ones. AHEAD adds 4.9M parameters to a frozen 7B OpenVLA and reaches 79 to 97% success across 20 dynamic simulation scenarios where the strongest baseline reaches 31 to 58%. On a physical UFactory xArm 7, AHEAD succeeds on 29/30 to 30/30 on three conveyor and rolling-ball tasks, 23/30 on paddle interception, and 19/30 on projectile catching where every baseline scores 0/30. Keywords: Vision-Language-Action Models, World Models, Manipulation
