瞬时（同时间步）一致性。在部分可观测性下，瞬时一致性是不够的：表示必须在时间上具有预测性。如果没有明确的时间约束，训练可能漂移或崩溃，导致弱的长时程结构——这些故障模式在记忆和导航密集型任务中显现。一种主流方法是使用像素解码器学习世界模型，如Dreamer中所示，其中重建产生丰富、控制有效的特征。其代价是建模负担：重建引入了繁重的生成目标，使优化复杂化，并且可能将容量分配给视觉细节丰富但与任务无关的方面。无解码器方法移除了像素解码器，直接训练表示以简化流程并提高效率。在本文中，我们引入了NE-Dreamer，这是一种无解码器世界模型，它通过直接优化其潜在表示中的时间预测对齐来学习。NE-Dreamer用一个简单而强大的目标取代了像素级重建：在每个时间步，一个时间Transformer预测序列中的下一个编码器嵌入，并且这个预测使用冗余减少度量（具体来说，在我们实现中是Barlow Twins）与实际的下一步嵌入对齐。通过将重点从同时间步匹配转移到下一步预测，NE-Dreamer学习时间连贯的潜在状态，而无需像素重建、数据增强。图1. DMLab基准总结。在匹配的计算和模型容量下（50M环境步；5个种子；12M参数），NE-Dreamer在DMLab Rooms记忆/导航任务上优于强大的基于解码器（DreamerV3）和无解码器世界模型基线（R2-Dreamer, DreamerPro）。从高维观测中进行的基于模型的强化学习（MBRL）取决于学习一个紧凑的潜在状态，以支持长时程预测和控制。这一要求在部分可观测性下变得更加重要：智能体必须随时间整合信息，而不是对单个帧做出反应。

stantaneous (same-timestep) agreement. Under partial observability, instantaneous agreement is not enough: the representation must be predictive across time. Without an explicit temporal constraint, training can drift or collapse, leading to weak long-horizon structure—failure modes that surface in memory- and navigation-heavy tasks. A dominant approach learns the world model with a pixel decoder, as in Dreamer, where reconstruction produces rich, control-effective features. The cost is modeling burden: reconstruction introduces a heavy generative objective, complicates optimization, and can allocate capacity to visually detailed but task-irrelevant aspects. Decoder-free methods remove the pixel decoder, training representations directly to simplify the pipeline and improve efficiency. In this paper, we introduce NE-Dreamer, a decoder-free world model that learns by directly optimizing for temporal predictive alignment in its latent representations. NEDreamer replaces pixel-level reconstruction with a simple yet powerful objective: at each timestep, a temporal transformer predicts the next encoder embedding in the sequence, and this prediction is aligned to the actual next-step embedding using a redundancy-reduction metric (specifically, Barlow Twins in our implementation). By shifting the focus from same-timestep matching to next-step prediction, NE-Dreamer learns temporally coherent latent states without the need for pixel reconstruction, data augmentation, Figure 1. DMLab Benchmark Summary. Under matched compute and model capacity (50M environment steps; 5 seeds; 12M parameters), NE-Dreamer outperforms strong decoder-based (DreamerV3) and decoder-free world-model baselines (R2-Dreamer, DreamerPro) on the DMLab Rooms memory/navigation tasks. Model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) from highdimensional observations hinges on learning a compact latent state that supports long-horizon prediction and control. This requirement becomes more important under partial observability: the agent must integrate information over time rather than react to a single frame.
