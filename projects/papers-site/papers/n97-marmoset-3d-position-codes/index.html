原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

动物位置的神经编码对于空间导航和认知至关重要，但灵长类动物大脑的三维定位系统仍不甚清楚。通过将无线多通道录音结合在3D空间中的自由觅食任务，研究人员在额叶皮层和海马体中分别识别出42%和24%的神经元作为位置细胞。这两个区域的位置细胞以相似的空间信息、稀疏性和稳定性编码3D位置，并且与非位置细胞相比，它们具有更强的功能连接，并且它们的定位场被一个模拟FC-HPC网络的RNN-AutoEncoder模型所重现。

原始摘要

The neural encoding of the animal's position is essential for spatial navigation and cognition, yet the three-dimensional positioning system in the primate brain remains poorly understood. By combining wireless multi-channel recordings from common marmosets with a free-foraging task in 3D space, the authors identified 42% and 24% neurons in the frontal cortex and hippocampus as place cells, respectively. Place cells in both regions encoded 3D position with similar spatial information, sparsity and stability, exhibited stronger functional connections than non-place cells, and their place fields were recapitulated by an RNN-AutoEncoder model simulating the FC-HPC network.
