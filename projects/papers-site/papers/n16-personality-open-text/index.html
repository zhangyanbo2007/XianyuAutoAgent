原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

当代人格评估严重依赖心理测量量表，这些量表虽然提供了效率，但有过度简化人格丰富性和情境性本质的风险。认识到这些局限性，本研究探索了使用商用生成式大型语言模型（LLMs），例如ChatGPT、Claude等，从开放式定性叙事中评估人格特质。在两个不同的样本和方法（自发思绪流和日常视频日记）中，我们使用了七个商用生成式LLMs来评估大五人格特质，实现了与自我报告测量相当或超越既定基准（例如，自我-他人一致性、生态瞬时评估和定制机器学习模型）的收敛性。尽管不同LLMs的结果有所差异，但我们发现使用跨模型的LLM平均得分与自我报告的一致性最强。此外，LLM生成的人格特质得分还展示了对日常行为和心理健康结果的预测效度。这种基于LLM的方法在定性数据的基础上实现了定量严谨性，并且无需专业培训即可轻松获取。重要的是，我们的研究结果也再次证实，人格无处不在地表达，它存在于我们的思绪流中，并融入我们日常生活的方方面面。这些结果鼓励更广泛地采用生成式LLMs进行心理评估，并且——鉴于新一代工具的出现——强调了个体叙事作为可靠心理洞察来源的价值。图2：LLM对思绪流提示（研究1，左图）和日常日记（研究2，右图）中人格的估计与自我报告调查的相关性，并与自我-他人调查一致性的元分析估计（Connelley and Ones, 2010）进行比较。

原始摘要

Contemporary personality assessment relies heavily on psychometric scales, which offer efficiency but risk oversimplifying the rich and contextual nature of personality. Recognizing these limitations, this study explores the use of commercially available generative large language models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, Claude and so on, to assess personality traits from open-ended qualitative narratives. Across two distinct samples and methodologies (spontaneous streams of thought and daily video diaries), we used seven commercial, generative LLMs to score Big-Five personality traits, achieving convergence with self-report measures comparable to or exceeding established benchmarks (for example, self–other agreement, ecological momentary assessment, and bespoke machine learning models). Although results differed across different LLMs, we found that using the average LLM score across models provided the strongest agreement with self-report. Further, LLM-generated trait scores also demonstrated predictive validity regarding daily behaviours and mental health outcomes. This LLM-based approach achieved quantitative rigour based on qualitative data and is easily accessible without specialized training. Importantly, our findings also reaffirm that personality is expressed ubiquitously, in that it is carried in the stream of our thoughts and is woven into the fabric of our daily lives. These results encourage broader adoption of generative LLMs for psychological assessment and—given the new generation of tools—stress the value of idiographic narratives as reliable sources of psychological insight.
