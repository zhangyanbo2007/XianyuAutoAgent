问题/背景

结果/证据

中文对照翻译

BenDFM：一种用于钣金折弯可制造性评估的分类法和合成CAD数据集 摘要 早期预测CAD设计的可制造性，包括可行性和所需工作量，是面向制造设计（DFM）的一个关键目标。尽管CAD深度学习取得了进展，并在制造工艺选择中得到广泛应用，但针对特定工艺预测可制造性的基于学习的方法仍然有限。限制进展的两个关键挑战是：先前工作中可制造性定义的不一致性以及由此产生的相关学习目标的不一致性，以及合适数据集的稀缺性。现有标签差异很大：它们可能反映内在设计约束或取决于特定的制造能力（例如可用工具），并且范围从离散可行性检查到连续复杂性度量。此外，工业数据集通常只包含可制造零件，为不可行情况提供的信号很少，而现有合成数据集则侧重于简单几何形状和减材工艺。为了解决这些空白，我们提出了一个可制造性指标分类法，沿配置依赖性和测量类型轴进行划分，从而更清晰地界定泛化能力和学习目标。接下来，我们介绍了BenDFM，这是第一个用于钣金折弯可制造性评估的合成数据集。BenDFM包含20,000个零件，既有可制造的也有不可制造的，通过工艺感知折弯仿真生成，提供折叠和展开的几何形状以及跨分类法的多个可制造性标签，从而能够系统地研究以前未探索的基于学习的DFM挑战。我们在BenDFM上对两种最先进的3D学习架构进行了基准测试，结果表明，捕获零件表面之间关系的基于图的表示实现了更好的准确性，并且预测依赖于特定制造设置的指标仍然更具挑战性。综上所述，该分类法和BenDFM数据集为工业设计工作流程中深度学习辅助的DFM迈出了重要一步。

原始摘要

A BSTRACT Predicting the manufacturability of CAD designs early, in terms of both feasibility and required effort, is a key goal of Design for Manufacturing (DFM). Despite advances in deep learning for CAD and its widespread use in manufacturing process selection, learning-based approaches for predicting manufacturability within a specific process remain limited. Two key challenges limit progress: inconsistency across prior work in how manufacturability is defined and consequently in the associated learning targets, and a scarcity of suitable datasets. Existing labels vary significantly: they may reflect intrinsic design constraints or depend on specific manufacturing capabilities (such as available tools), and they range from discrete feasibility checks to continuous complexity measures. Furthermore, industrial datasets typically contain only manufacturable parts, offering little signal for infeasible cases, while existing synthetic datasets focus on simple geometries and subtractive processes. To address these gaps, we propose a taxonomy of manufacturability metrics along the axes of configuration dependence and measurement type, allowing clearer scoping of generalizability and learning objectives. Next, we introduce BenDFM, the first synthetic dataset for manufacturability assessment in sheet metal bending. BenDFM contains 20,000 parts, both manufacturable and unmanufacturable, generated with process-aware bending simulations, providing both folded and unfolded geometries and multiple manufacturability labels across the taxonomy, enabling systematic study of previously unexplored learning-based DFM challenges. We benchmark two state-of-theart 3D learning architectures on BenDFM, showing that graph-based representations that capture relationships between part surfaces achieve better accuracy, and that predicting metrics that depend on specific manufacturing setups remains more challenging. Together, the taxonomy and BenDFM dataset provide a significant step toward deep-learning-assisted DFM in industrial design workflows.
