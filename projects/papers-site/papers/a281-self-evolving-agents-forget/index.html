原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

LLM智能体在自主优化工作流、积累可重用技能、自训练其底层模型以及维护持久记忆方面的最新进展，使得相关系统成为可能。然而，我们发现这种自我演化通常是非单调的：适应新的任务分布会逐步降低在所有主要演化通道中先前获得的能力。我们将这种现象识别为自我演化下的能力侵蚀，并表明它在工作流、技能、模型和记忆演化中持续出现。为了缓解这个问题，我们提出了能力保持演化（CPE），这是一种通用的稳定化原则，它在持续适应过程中限制了破坏性的能力漂移。在所有四个演化维度上，CPE持续提高了保留能力稳定性，同时保持了适应性能。例如，在工作流演化中，CPE在GPT-5.1优化下将保留的简单任务性能从41.8%提高到52.8%，同时实现了更强的复杂任务适应性。我们的研究结果表明，稳定的长周期自我演化智能体不仅需要获取新能力，还需要在持续适应过程中明确保留先前学习到的能力。

原始摘要

Recent advances in LLM agents enable systems that autonomously refine workflows, accumulate reusable skills, self-train their underlying models, and maintain persistent memory. However, we show that such self-evolution is often nonmonotonic: adapting to new task distributions can progressively degrade previously acquired capabilities across all major evolution channels. We identify this phenomenon as capability erosion under self-evolution and show that it consistently emerges across workflow, skill, model, and memory evolution. To mitigate this issue, we propose Capability-Preserving Evolution (CPE), a general stabilization principle that constrains destructive capability drift during continual adaptation. Across all four evolution dimensions, CPE consistently improves retained capability stability while preserving adaptation performance. For example, in workflow evolution, CPE improves retained simple-task performance from 41.8% to 52.8% under GPT-5.1 optimization while simultaneously achieving stronger complex-task adaptation. Our findings suggest that stable long-horizon self-evolving agents require not only acquiring new capabilities, but also explicitly preserving previously learned ones during continual adaptation.
