中文对照翻译

关于通过学习的转移模型实现样本高效的广义规划广义规划研究构建解决方案策略，这些策略能够泛化到共享共同领域模型（由转移函数 γ : S × A → S 正式定义）的规划问题族。经典方法通过符号抽象和对 γ 的显式推理来实现这种泛化。相比之下，最近基于 Transformer 的规划器，例如 PlanGPT 和 Plansformer，在很大程度上将广义规划视为直接的动作序列预测，从而绕过了显式转移建模。尽管这些方法在同分布实例上有效，但它们通常需要大型数据集和模型尺寸，并且由于缺乏显式世界状态演化，在长时程设置中经常遭受状态漂移。在这项工作中，我们将广义规划表述为一个转移模型学习问题，其中一个神经网络模型显式地近似后继状态函数 γ̂ ≈ γ，并通过展开符号状态轨迹来生成规划。该模型不直接预测动作，而是自回归地预测中间世界状态，从而将领域动力学学习为一个隐式世界模型。为了研究尺寸不变泛化和样本效率，我们系统地评估了多种状态表示和神经网络架构，包括关系图编码。我们的结果表明，在多个领域中，学习显式转移模型比直接动作序列预测能产生更高的域外满意规划成功率，同时以显著更少的训练实例和更小的模型实现了这些收益。这是在 ICAPS 2026 上以相同标题接受的一篇短论文的扩展版本。

原始摘要

Generalized planning studies the construction of solution strategies that generalize across families of planning problems sharing a common domain model, formally defined by a transition function γ : S × A → S. Classical approaches achieve such generalization through symbolic abstractions and explicit reasoning over γ. In contrast, recent Transformer-based planners, such as PlanGPT and Plansformer, largely cast generalized planning as direct actionsequence prediction, bypassing explicit transition modeling. While effective on in-distribution instances, these approaches typically require large datasets and model sizes, and often suffer from state drift in long-horizon settings due to the absence of explicit world-state evolution. In this work, we formulate generalized planning as a transition-model learning problem, in which a neural model explicitly approximates the successor-state function γ̂ ≈ γ and generates plans by rolling out symbolic state trajectories. Instead of predicting actions directly, the model autoregressively predicts intermediate world states, thereby learning the domain dynamics as an implicit world model. To study size-invariant generalization and sample efficiency, we systematically evaluate multiple state representations and neural architectures, including relational graph encodings. Our results show that learning explicit transition models yields higher out-of-distribution satisficing-plan success than direct action-sequence prediction in multiple domains, while achieving these gains with significantly fewer training instances and smaller models. This is an extended version of a short paper accepted at ICAPS 2026 under the same title.
