中文对照翻译

标题：星形胶质细胞通过可塑性网络连接特定脑区 传统上，神经元轴突被认为是脑区之间功能连接的主要介质。然而，星形胶质细胞介导的通信作用在很大程度上被低估了。星形胶质细胞通过间隙连接相互通信，但这种通信的范围和特异性仍知之甚少。星形胶质细胞间隙连接对于记忆形成、突触可塑性、神经元信号协调以及视觉和运动关键期的关闭至关重要。这些发现表明，这种形式的通信对于中枢神经系统的正常发育和功能至关重要。尽管星形胶质细胞间隙连接网络很重要，但对其研究一直充满挑战。当前的方法，例如切片电生理学，会破坏网络连接并因组织损伤引入伪影。在这里，我们开发了一种基于载体的方法，该方法在清醒、行为中的动物体内标记通过星形胶质细胞间隙连接流动的分子，以克服这些局限性。随后，我们使用全脑组织透明化技术对这些完整的、三维的星形胶质细胞网络进行成像。我们发现多个星形胶质细胞网络穿过小鼠大脑。这些网络选择性地连接特定区域，而不是不加区分地扩散，并且在大小和组织结构上各不相同。我们观察到局限于单个脑区的局部网络，以及在半球之间稳健互连多个区域的长程网络，这些网络通常表现出与已知神经元网络不同的模式。我们还证明，在感觉剥夺后，成年大脑中的星形胶质细胞网络会发生结构重组。这些发现揭示了一种由间隙连接偶联的星形胶质细胞可塑性网络介导的远距离脑区通信模式。

原始摘要

Neuronal axons have traditionally been considered to be the primary mediators of functional connectivity among brain regions. However, the role of astrocyte-mediated communication has been largely underappreciated. Astrocytes communicate with one another through gap junctions, but the extent and specificity of this communication remain poorly understood. Astrocyte gap junctions are necessary for memory formation , synaptic plasticity , coordination of neuronal signalling , and closing the visual and motor critical periods . These findings indicate that this form of communication is essential for proper central nervous system development and function. Despite the importance of astrocyte gap junctional networks, studying them has been challenging. Current methods such as slice electrophysiology disrupt network connectivity and introduce artefacts due to tissue damage. Here, we developed a vector-based approach that labels molecules as they are fluxed by astrocyte gap junctions in awake, behaving animals to overcome these limitations. We then used whole-brain tissue clearing to image these intact, three-dimensional astrocyte networks. We show that multiple astrocyte networks traverse the mouse brain. These networks selectively connect specific regions, rather than diffusing indiscriminately, and vary in size and organization. We observe local networks that are confined to single brain regions and long-range networks that robustly interconnect multiple regions across hemispheres, often exhibiting patterns distinct from known neuronal networks. We also demonstrate that astrocyte networks undergo structural reorganization in the adult brain after sensory deprivation. These findings reveal a mode of communication between distant brain regions that is mediated by plastic networks of gap junction-coupled astrocytes.
