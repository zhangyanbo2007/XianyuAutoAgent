原始摘要与中文对照

中文对照翻译

Synthesizer：化学感知机器学习实现纳米晶生长的精确控制。对纳米晶合成的精确和可重现控制对于定制光学性质至关重要，但在卤化物钙钛矿中仍然是一个长期存在的挑战。本文介绍了一种可广泛采用的机器学习引导框架——Synthesizer，它将高斯过程回归和贝叶斯优化与化学感知分子编码以及系统特征工程相结合。这一进展并非在于新的算法，而在于将可解释的机器学习工具转化为一个实用的台式平台，用于在环境条件下进行纳米晶优化。以CsPbBr3作为模型系统，实现了光致发光峰调谐（430 nm至520 nm）的纳米级精度，并通过横向限制控制实现了低至70 meV的基准窄线宽，以及与表面陷阱密度相关的稳健光致发光量子产率优化。绘制跨多种反溶剂的二维参数空间（Cs/PbBr2和反溶剂/PbBr2比率）实现了预测性优化，并确定反溶剂/PbBr2比率是一个此前未被充分认识的机制参数，为反溶剂加速的纳米晶生长提供了定量基础。跨不同化学空间（包括醇类和环戊酮）的迁移测试证实了其对未见分子的普适性，而应用于CsPbI3则展示了其对新材料体系的扩展。这些结果建立了一个可采用的平台，用于数据高效、不确定性感知合成设计，为加速卤化物钙钛矿以外的材料发现提供了可重现的途径。

原始摘要

Precise and reproducible control over nanocrystal synthesis is essential for tailoring optical properties, yet remains a long-standing challenge in halide perovskites. A broadly adoptable machine learning–guided framework, the Synthesizer, is introduced that combines Gaussian Process regression and Bayesian optimization with chemistry-aware molecular encodings and systematic feature engineering. Rather than new algorithms, the advance lies in translating interpretable machine learning tools into a practical, benchtop platform for nanocrystal optimization under ambient conditions. Using CsPbBr3 as a model system, nm-level precision in photoluminescence peak tuning (430 nm to 520 nm) is achieved, along with benchmark narrow linewidths down to 70 meV via lateral conﬁnement control, and robust photoluminescence quantum yield optimization linked to surface trap density. Mapping the two-dimensional parameter space (Cs/PbBr2 and antisolvent/PbBr2 ratios) across multiple antisolvents enables predictive optimization and identiﬁes the antisolvent/PbBr2 ratio as a previously underappreciated mechanistic parameter, oﬀering a quantitative basis for antisolvent-accelerated nanocrystal growth. Transfer tests across distinct chemical spaces, including alcohols and cyclopentanone, conﬁrm generalizability to unseen molecules, while application to CsPbI3 demonstrates extension to new material systems. These results establish an adoption-ready platform for data-eﬃcient, uncertainty-aware synthesis design, providing reproducible pathways to accelerate materials discovery beyond halide perovskites.
