中文对照翻译

数字健康领域的科学发现需要将可穿戴设备产生的连续生理信号转化为具有临床可操作性的生物标志物。我们引入了CoDaS（AI协同数据科学家），这是一个多智能体系统，它将生物标志物发现构建为一个迭代过程，结合了假设生成、统计分析、对抗性验证和基于文献的推理，并在人类监督下使用大规模可穿戴数据集。在总计9,279个参与者观测值的三项队列研究中，CoDaS识别出41个精神健康候选数字生物标志物和25个代谢结果候选数字生物标志物，每个都经过了涵盖重复性、稳定性、鲁棒性和判别力的内部验证。在两个独立的抑郁症队列中，CoDaS在两个数据集中都发现了与昼夜节律不稳定性相关的特征，这体现在睡眠持续时间变异性（DWB，𝜌 = 0.252，𝑝 < 0.001）和睡眠开始时间变异性（GLOBEM，𝜌 = 0.126，𝑝 < 0.001）中。在一个代谢队列中，CoDaS推导出了心血管健康指数（步数/静息心率；𝜌 = −0.374，𝑝 < 0.001），并恢复了已建立的临床关联，包括肝功能比率（AST/ALT；𝜌 = −0.375，𝑝 < 0.001），这是胰岛素抵抗的一个已知相关因素。将CoDaS衍生的特征与人口统计学变量结合，预测性能得到了适度但持续的改善，其中抑郁症的交叉验证Δ𝑅2增加了0.040，胰岛素抵抗的交叉验证Δ𝑅2增加了0.021。这些发现表明，CoDaS能够从大规模可穿戴数据中系统地、可追溯地生成和优先排序生物标志物发现假设。

原始摘要

Scientific discovery in digital health requires converting continuous physiological signals from wearable devices into clinically actionable biomarkers. We introduce CoDaS (AI Co-Data-Scientist), a multi-agent system that structures biomarker discovery as an iterative process combining hypothesis generation, statistical analysis, adversarial validation, and literature-grounded reasoning with human oversight using large-scale wearable datasets. Across three cohorts totaling 9,279 participant-observations, CoDaS identified 41 candidate digital biomarkers for mental health and 25 for metabolic outcomes, each subjected to an internal validation battery spanning replication, stability, robustness, and discriminative power. Across two independent depression cohorts, CoDaS surfaced circadian instability-related features in both datasets, reflected in sleep duration variability (DWB, 𝜌 = 0.252, 𝑝 < 0.001) and sleep onset variability (GLOBEM, 𝜌 = 0.126, 𝑝 < 0.001). In a metabolic cohort, CoDaS derived a cardiovascular fitness index (steps/resting heart rate; 𝜌 = −0.374, 𝑝 < 0.001), and recovered established clinical associations, including the hepatic function ratio (AST/ALT; 𝜌 = −0.375, 𝑝 < 0.001), a known correlate of insulin resistance. Incorporating CoDaS-derived features alongside demographic variables led to modest but consistent improvements in predictive performance, with cross-validated Δ𝑅2 increases of 0.040 for depression and 0.021 for insulin resistance. These findings suggest that CoDaS enables systematic and traceable hypothesis generation and prioritization for biomarker discovery from large-scale wearable data.
